Every Global Capability Center reaches a point where what made it work starts to hold it back.

Read on

It rarely shows up as a single problem. It shows up as a pattern.

Decisions that used to be made quickly start climbing for approval. Strong people leave without quite saying why. Delivery still holds — but on individual effort more than on design. Managers stretch past what the structure was built to carry.

None of these is a crisis on its own. Together, they are a signal: the organization is being asked to carry more than its structure was built to hold.

This is the post-build stage. It is not a failure of the build. It is what happens when the build succeeds.

The GCC Scalability Index

These are not eight separate problems. They are eight places where the same strain shows up.

The GCC Scalability Index is a way of seeing a center's structure clearly — locating exactly where growth is being constrained, before the constraint becomes visible to everyone else.

01Operating ModelWhether the center owns its decisions, or still sends them upward.
02Global StandingWhether the center is regarded as a strategic peer, or only a delivery arm.
03Employer Brand & Talent AttractionWhether the right people seek the center out, or have to be chased.
04Cross-Border GovernanceWhether authority is real and clear, or negotiated case by case.
05Organization DesignWhether the structure carries the work, or the work carries the structure.
06HR & Compliance MaturityWhether the people foundation is solid, or quietly exposed.
07Workforce Cost StructureWhether cost is governed by design, or drifting by default.
08Leadership CapabilityWhether the center builds leaders, or relies on the ones it has.
